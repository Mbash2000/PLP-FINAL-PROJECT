Nigeria Agricultural Support Programmes

Explore some major programmes and initiatives supporting farmers across Nigeria.

Anchor Borrowers’ Programme (ABP)

The ABP by Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN) supports smallholder farmers with loans/inputs to produce key crops — especially rice, maize, cassava and more. This initiative helps boost domestic food production and reduce reliance on food imports. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Who Can Apply

Smallholder farmers involved in priority crops, organized as cooperatives or individual small farms. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Benefits

  • Access to subsidized inputs (seeds, fertilizers)
  • Low-interest loan/credit support
  • Support for production of staple crops — boosting food security

Growth Enhancement Support Scheme (GESS)

GESS is an input‑subsidy initiative that provides farmers with subsidized fertilizers, improved seeds and agro‑inputs via registration and e‑wallet support. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Purpose & Reach

Intended especially for smallholder farmers, GESS aims to increase yield, boost agricultural productivity and make inputs affordable. Between 2012–2014, millions of farmers benefited. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

NIRSAL Plc (Nigeria Incentive‑Based Risk Sharing System for Agricultural Lending)

NIRSAL helps de-risk agricultural lending in Nigeria — offering credit‑risk guarantees, facilitating affordable loans, and providing technical assistance to farmers and agro‑processors. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

Features

  • Credit‑risk guarantee for banks lending to agriculture
  • Support for cooperatives and smallholders without traditional collateral
  • Technical assistance, capacity building, and value‑chain support

Private‑Sector Agricultural Development Scheme (P‑AADS)

The Central Bank of Nigeria initiated P‑AADS to support large‑scale agricultural production through land clearing, infrastructure facilitation, and financing for agro‑processors and serious farmers. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

Target Beneficiaries

Agro‑processors, commercial farmers, investors in agriculture seeking land clearing, mechanization, and scale production support. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

National Programme for Food Security (NPFS)

The Federal Ministry of Agriculture and Food Security (FMAFS) administers NPFS to boost food production and enhance rural livelihoods across Nigeria. :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}

Objective

Improve national & household food security, increase agricultural productivity, provide extension services and support to rural farmers. :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}
